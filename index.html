How to Play Backgammon

1. The board has 24 points arranged in two rows:
• Top row: Points 13–24 (left-to-right)
• Bottom row: Points 12–1 (left-to-right)

2. The standard starting position is used:
• White: 2 checkers on point 24, 5 on point 13, 3 on point 8, and 5 on point 6
• Black: 2 checkers on point 1, 5 on point 12, 3 on point 17, and 5 on point 19

3. White's checkers move from higher-numbered points toward point 1; Black's move in the opposite direction.

4. Click "Roll Dice" to roll the dice. The dice indicate how many points you can move. Doubles grant four moves.

5. Click on a point that contains your checkers to select a move. If more than one move is possible from that point, a prompt will ask you to choose a die value.

6. Bearing off (removing checkers) is allowed when all your checkers are in your home board (points 1–6 for White).

7. After your move, the bot (Black) moves automatically.

8. Enjoy this simplified, modern take on classic Backgammon!
